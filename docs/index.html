Regenerative Potential Index – Identification and Assessment in Amsterdam

2024, PhD Research

The Regenerative Potential Index (RPI) is an evaluation framework for assessing the regenerative potential of urban areas — applicable to both public and private sectors. It aggregates multiple indicators across four dimensions — Economic, Environmental, Political and Social — into a single composite score (0–100) per territorial unit per year.

This case study applies the RPI methodology to the districts of the municipality of Amsterdam, tracking performance across a decade of data (2015–2024) and projecting three future scenarios to 2030 and 2050: Constrained, Business-As-Usual, and Optimistic. All indicators are normalised using a z-score approach (k=15), with direction adjustment and neutralisation for near-constant series.

The visualisations below are interactive — hover over data points to inspect values, click the legend to isolate specific districts or scenarios, and use the dimension buttons to switch between the four RPI dimensions.
